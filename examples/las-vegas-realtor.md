### MAIN CONTENT

**Section 1: HOA Transfer Fees (1:30-3:00)**
"Las Vegas has one of the highest HOA rates in the country. Over 70% of communities here have a homeowners association — and most of them charge a transfer fee when a property sells. This fee is NOT included in your closing cost estimate from the lender. It typically runs $250 to $500, but I've seen them as high as $900 in Summerlin communities. Always ask your agent to request the HOA documents up front.

Here's what you do: before you make an offer, ask the listing agent for the HOA transfer fee schedule. Get it in writing. Then add it to your closing cost spreadsheet."

*B-roll:* HOA sign at a community entrance
*On-screen text:* "HOA TRANSFER FEE: $250-$900 — Ask BEFORE making an offer"

**Section 2: Home Warranty (3:00-4:30)**
"Nevada doesn't require sellers to provide a home warranty, but you should negotiate for one. A standard 1-year warranty in Las Vegas runs $400-$600 for the buyer. Why does this matter? Because our HVAC systems work incredibly hard — 4-5 months of 100+ degree heat takes a toll. A warranty that covers HVAC replacement pays for itself after one incident. If the seller won't cover it, factor it into your budget."

*B-roll:* HVAC unit on a rooftop
*On-screen text:* "HOME WARRANTY: $400-600 — Negotiate seller-paid, not buyer-paid"

**Section 3: Pool Inspection (4:30-5:30)**
"Roughly 30% of Las Vegas homes have pools. A standard home inspection does NOT cover pools — that's a separate inspection at $150-300. Skip it and you might be buying a pool that needs $5,000 in resurfacing or a pump that's at end of life. If the home has a pool, always add this to your inspection contingency."

*On-screen text:* "POOL INSPECTION: $150-300 — Separate from standard home inspection"

**MID-VIDEO CTA (5:30):**
"If you've learned something valuable so far, hit the like button — it helps this video reach more Las Vegas buyers who need this information. And tell me in the comments: are you currently in the search process, or still in the research phase? I read every comment."

**Section 4: Move-In Utilities Deposit (5:45-7:00)**
"NV Energy, Southwest Gas, and the water authority all require deposits from new accounts. If you're coming from out of state, expect to pay $100-$400 in utility deposits your first month. Budget for it so it doesn't catch you off guard right after paying closing costs."

**Section 5: Immediate Repairs (7:00-8:30)**
"Even with a clean inspection, there are almost always first-week repairs. I tell every buyer: budget $2,000-$5,000 for immediate move-in expenses — door locks changed ($150), garage remote programming ($75-200), touch-up paint, small fixes the seller didn't address. This isn't a surprise if you plan for it."

### OUTRO (8:30-10:00)
